Security & Hardening

My approach is based on practical enterprise security: least privilege, hardened baselines, controlled change, and fast incident response — aligned with strong industry practices (e.g., CIS-style baselines, secure-by-default configuration, MFA-first access, logging & monitoring, and continuous patching).

Identity & Access Controls

  • Join/Move/Leave lifecycle with approvals & role-based access
  • MFA enforcement and privileged access hygiene
  • Least privilege: remove standing admin, audit elevated rights
  • Access reviews: periodic review of groups/roles and stale accounts

System Hardening Baseline

  • Secure configuration: services, ports, firewall rules, and endpoint policies
  • Account policies: password policy, lockout policy, and privileged role controls
  • OS & application patch management with rollback awareness
  • Secure remote access: VPN / jump-host / RDP controls (where applicable)

Logging, Monitoring & Response

  • Central logging mindset: authentication, admin actions, policy changes
  • Alert triage and incident handling with clear runbooks
  • RCA after incidents: fix root causes and update controls
  • Operational discipline: change windows, approvals, and documentation

Network & Security Operations

  • Basic network operations: routing, switching, DNS/DHCP fundamentals
  • Network edge controls: APs, firewall rules, and segmentation concepts
  • Vulnerability remediation: patching + configuration corrections
  • Backup & recovery readiness aligned to business criticality
